<h2 style="margin: 60px 0px 10px;">Short Bio</h2>

I am a second-year Cognitive Science PhD student at [UC Irvine (UCI)](https://www.cogsci.uci.edu/), work with [Prof. Jeffrey Rouder](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=W5inQnkAAAAJ). I obtained my B.S. in NYU Shanghai doubling in Neuroscience and Data Science, advised by [Prof. Zhonglin Lu](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=dBz3AIEAAAAJ&hl=en).  

I like mathematics and statistics, and I am particularly drawn to the quantitative description of complex phenomena—including the human brain. My research focuses on individual differences, Bayesian inference, and hierarchical modeling, with an emphasis on using advanced statistical methods to advance research in cognitive science.

Research is only one part of my life. Outside of academia, I am a quite good rhythm game player, particularly in osu!mania, Arcaea, and Chunithm. I also enjoy playing basketball and tennis on an almost daily basis. In addition, I enjoy LOL, Steam, cooking, playing Texas Hold’em, and exploring the philosophy of mind. More broadly, I am drawn to anything that possesses its own aesthetic value.
